Planning Guides

Written workflows for choosing the right starting point, understanding why each calculator matters, documenting assumptions, and turning rough planning inputs into report-ready design notes.

Use guides when you need the workflow, not just the calculation.

Calculator pages answer a specific question. Planning guides explain the sequence around that question: what to confirm first, what can break the result, which follow-up checks matter, and what should be captured before the work becomes a client-ready report.

Featured calculator guide: PoE Budget

Start here when planning cameras, wireless access points, VoIP phones, intercoms, or access-control devices powered from a switch. The guide explains how device count, wattage, future growth, and headroom fit together before the calculator is used.

2. Add real load and growth Use rated draw, expansion allowance, and realistic switch budget.
3. Check headroom before selection Document pressure, reserve, and whether the design needs a larger switch.
