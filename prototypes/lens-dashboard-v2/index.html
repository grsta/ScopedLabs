Engineering Interpretation

At 80 ft with a target width of 20 ft and a 6.40 mm sensor, the raw geometry drives the design into a long-range focal length class.

Dominant Constraint

Focal demand is the dominant limiter. This pushes the scene toward a narrow field of view and reduces layout flexibility.

Corrective Guidance / Path to Healthy

Why this is Risk

The required focal length is being driven upward by the distance-to-width relationship and the detail requirement.

Likely Drivers

  • Long standoff distance (80.0 ft)
  • Narrow target width (20.0 ft)
  • High detail requirement
  • Small sensor size (6.40 mm)

Possible Planning Actions

  • Move the camera closer to the target area
  • Increase the target width or scene coverage
  • Use a larger sensor format
  • Use multiple cameras to cover the area
  • Re-evaluate identification/detail requirement

Healthy Target

Bring Lens Selection Pressure back into the comfort band while preserving the required identification/detail objective.

Follow-up Checks

  • Validate final field of view on site plan
  • Confirm identification distance requirements
  • Check manufacturer lens availability
  • Verify sensor format compatibility

Revision Triggers

  • Change in mounting distance
  • Change in target width / scene coverage
  • Change in sensor or camera model
  • Change in detail / identification requirement
